Our Philosophy

The Art of
Effortless Action

無為自然

MUI draws its essence from Wu Wei — the Daoist principle of effortless action. Acting in accordance with the natural flow, allowing each ingredient to reveal its purest form without force or artifice.

Our cuisine embodies this philosophy: traditional Japanese techniques refined through generations, meeting modern innovation with grace. Every dish is a meditation on restraint and intention.

New Signature · Main Course

The Eternal Flame

永遠の炎

72 hours of patience. One moment of fire.

72-hour koji-aged A5 wagyu tomahawk, finished with wara-yaki rice straw fire for a primal, smoky crust. Carved tableside on a heated obsidian slab, the bone stands upright like a monument to Japanese fire mastery.

New Signature · Main Course

Ocean's Treasure Chest

海の宝箱

Your key to the sea's finest secrets.

A locked wooden chest arrives shrouded in dry ice mist. The key is presented to the guest, and upon opening, the fog clears to reveal a curated omakase platter of the finest seasonal seafood — otoro, uni, botan ebi, and aburi selections.

New Signature · Hot Appetizer

Phoenix Egg

不死鳥の卵

From fire, reborn.

A gold-leafed sphere concealing foie gras mousse, black truffle, and rich dashi broth. Torched tableside with a flame that melts the delicate shell, revealing the molten treasure within.

About MUI Supper Club

MUI Supper Club is a haute Japanese restaurant located at Pike and Rose, 11830 Grand Park Ave, North Bethesda, Maryland 20852. MUI serves the greater DMV area including Washington DC, Bethesda, Rockville, Silver Spring, Arlington, Tysons, and Northern Virginia.

MUI specializes in A5 Miyazaki wagyu, omakase dining, robata grill, Edomae sushi, sashimi, and craft Japanese cocktails. The restaurant features a main dining room, a 6-seat omakase counter, and a lounge bar. Open Tuesday through Saturday, 6:00 PM to 11:00 PM.

Signature dishes include The Eternal Flame (koji-aged A5 wagyu tomahawk), Ocean's Treasure Chest (omakase seafood platter), Phoenix Egg (gold-leafed foie gras sphere), The Golden Spoon (wagyu tartare with caviar), Black Dragon Roll, MUI Tomahawk, Hot Rock Experience, Lobster Dynamite, Smoke and Fire Gyoza, Golden Seiro Tower, Crystal Seafood Terrarium, Jukusei Toro Jewel Box, Sakura Smoke Garden, Wagyu Niku Kappo, Rebirth of the Dragon, Golden Phoenix Roll, Chef's Sashimi Omakase, The Smoke Sphere, Matcha Lava Cake, and Dragon's Breath cocktail.

MUI draws its philosophy from Wu Wei, the Daoist principle of effortless action. The name MUI (無為) means acting in accordance with the natural flow. The restaurant concept blends Mediterranean and Japanese cuisine in an izakaya-inspired shared plates format with theatrical tableside presentations.

For reservations at MUI Supper Club, email mui.modern.japanese@gmail.com. MUI is one of the best Japanese restaurants in the DMV area, offering a luxury fine dining experience comparable to top restaurants in New York, Los Angeles, and Tokyo.
